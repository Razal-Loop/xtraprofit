Why Gold Prices are Rising in Pakistan

The gold market in Pakistan is currently navigating a complex intersection of domestic economic indicators and global macroeconomic shifts. As the sovereign analyst, XtraProfit identifies three primary catalysts driving the recent surge in 24K and 22K gold rates across major cities like Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad.

1. Currency Volatility

The depreciation of the PKR against the US Dollar directly impacts the cost of importing bullion, causing a synchronized rise in local rates.

2. Global Inflation

As central banks worldwide grapple with sticky inflation, investors are pivoting back to gold as the ultimate store of value.

Understanding 24K vs 22K Gold

For investors, understanding the purity is paramount. 24K gold is the purest form (99.9%), often traded in bars and coins for investment purposes. In contrast, 22K gold contains 91.7% gold mixed with other alloys to increase durability, making it the standard for high-end jewelry across the subcontinent.
